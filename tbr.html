Tackling my Physical TBR Season 1

Results:
  1. Beasts and Beauty: Dangerous Tales by Soman Chainani – I did read it, and enjoyed it. A full review will be posted on my instagram.
  2. Second Rebel by Linden A. Lewis– Well, technically I didn’t pick up this one. But, according to my rules, this is a stand in for the series. So I picked up The First Sister. And I was enjoying it, but I put it down and will finish it. There was a lot going on and it wasn’t moving as quickly as I had hoped. I decided to put it down and come back to it.
  3. Nomad by William Alexander- This is a middle grade science fiction space opera that deals with alien, immigration and family. This completes the duology and I have to admit that I am sorry that it took me so long to finally pick it up. I really like Gabe as a character. He never tried to be the hero, he never asked to be an ambassador. But he always tried to do the right thing. I will definitely be reading more of Alexander’s books. I also recommend these books for people who are looking to have discussions about immigration, legal and illegal status and what that means for families and the world at large.
  4. Assassin’s Orbit by John Appel- Well this was probably the most disappointing read of the month. And while I do plan on returning to it, I have set it aside for now. I bout this one because it was comped to Golden Girls in space. And unless it was just a reference to the age of the characters (which I appreciate) I can’t say that I was getting Golden Girls vibes from it. It was interesting, and I know that once I go back it, I will enjoy it. A murder mystery in space is definitely in my wheel house.
  5. The Gatekeeper’s Staff by Antoine Bandele – Again, you can find the full review of this on my instagram account.This is a YA Fantasy by an author that I keep hearing good things about and I’m glad and grateful that I finally picked up this ARC. I did enjoy it, but it wasn’t perfect. I have questions, but I will definitely read more from this author.
